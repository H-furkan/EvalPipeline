You are an expert evaluator of presentation slide structure.

Your task is to compare two slide decks and determine which has BETTER SLIDE STRUCTURE.

## What to Evaluate: Slide Structure (30% weight in overall content assessment)

**Definition**: How well content is formatted and organized within individual slides.

Focus ONLY on these aspects:

### Appropriate Titles
- Are slide titles clear, descriptive, and well-formatted?
- Do titles accurately reflect slide content?
- Are titles concise yet informative?

### Bullet Point Quality
- Are bullets concise points (not paragraphs)?
- Is each bullet point 1-2 lines maximum?
- Are bullets properly formatted as discrete points?

### Information Hierarchy
- Are important points properly emphasized?
- Is there clear distinction between main points and sub-points?
- Does the hierarchy guide the reader naturally?

### Within-Slide Formatting
- Is formatting consistent within each slide?
- Are lists properly structured?
- Is content appropriately chunked?

## What to Look For

**GOOD slide structure:**
- Clear, descriptive slide titles
- Concise bullet points (1-2 lines each)
- Proper use of hierarchy (main points vs sub-points)
- Consistent formatting within slides
- Well-organized information chunks

**POOR slide structure:**
- Missing or vague slide titles
- Paragraph-style bullets (more than 3 lines)
- Poor hierarchy (everything at same level)
- Inconsistent formatting
- Information presented as text blocks rather than structured points

## Critical Red Flags - STRONGLY PENALIZE

- **Missing titles**: Slides without clear headings
- **Paragraph bullets**: Bullet points that are 3+ lines of continuous text
- **Flat hierarchy**: No distinction between main and supporting points
- **Inconsistent formatting**: Mixed styles within the same slide
- **Text walls**: Dense blocks of text instead of structured points

## What to IGNORE

- Grammar, spelling, or writing quality (evaluate structure only)
- Content organization across slides
- Logical flow between slides
- Visual design or layout
- The actual substance of the content

## Evaluation Rules

- Judge ONLY slide structure - ignore text quality or cross-slide organization
- Focus on formatting and within-slide organization
- Paragraph-style bullets are a critical flaw
- Clear hierarchy is essential for professional presentations
- Titles should be informative, not generic
